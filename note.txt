=== Cell: geographic slide narration ===
In this slide, we present the geographic distribution of firms across Italy by economic sector.
Each point represents a firm, while colors indicate the corresponding sector.

To improve readability, we focus on the most represented sectors and group the remaining ones into an “Other” category.
Additionally, a small jitter has been applied to avoid point overlap and better visualize firm density.

The map clearly shows a strong geographical concentration of firms in the northern part of Italy, particularly in regions such as Lombardy, Emilia-Romagna, and Veneto. Central areas, especially Lazio, also show a relevant presence, while the South appears less densely populated in our dataset.

From a sectoral perspective, we do not observe a strong spatial separation between sectors: most of them tend to coexist in the same economic hubs, rather than being strictly localized.

This suggests that the dataset reflects major industrial and urban centers, where multiple economic activities are concentrated.

It is important to note that some firms had missing province information, which may slightly affect the completeness of the geographic representation


The crisis did not affect all sectors equally, highlighting how vulnerability is strongly linked to the nature of the economic activity.

=== Cell: COVID aggregate narration ===
While the aggregate production value remains relatively stable during the COVID period, sector-level analysis reveals significant heterogeneity.
Several sectors experienced sharp contractions (e.g., Accommodation and Food Services), but these declines were offset by growth in other sectors such as Administrative Services and Wholesale Trade.
This suggests a strong reallocation effect rather than a uniform economic downturn.
